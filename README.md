## 💡 Executive Summary
This project analyzes multi-decade global healthcare data to understand the direct relationships between financial investments in healthcare and actual patient outcomes (such as life expectancy and infant mortality rates). It identifies key performance indicators (KPIs) across countries to highlight successful healthcare delivery systems and areas requiring infrastructure improvement.
------------------------------
## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills Demonstrated

* Tool: Power BI Desktop
* Data Engineering: Data Cleaning, Transformation (Power Query), Data Modeling
* Analytics: DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) for custom KPIs and trend analysis
* Data Visualization: KPI Cards, Time-Series Line Charts, Scatter Plots, Dual-Axis Combination Charts, and Ranked Bar Charts

------------------------------
## 📈 Key Insights Discovered

   1. Diminishing Returns on Spending: Increased healthcare spending strongly correlates with longer life expectancy up to a specific threshold, after which efficiency gains plateau.
   2. Infrastructure Direct Impact: Countries with a higher ratio of available physicians and hospital beds per capita see a sharp reduction in child and infant mortality rates.
   3. Gender Gap: Across a vast majority of tracked nations, female life expectancy consistently outpaces male life expectancy, regardless of the country's economic standing.
